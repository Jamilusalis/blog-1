What to do if you spill fluids on your computer.

4-5 minutes

NOTE : Avoid the temptation to check on your computer while it's still on. You could cause further damage it.

  • Shut down – the first thing that you need to do is to power off your laptop. Don’t waste time doing it the regular way (from the Start Menu); simply put your finger on the power button and keep it pressed for 4 seconds to force a quick shut down.
  • Power outlet – if the laptop was plugged into a power outlet, take out the power chord first from your laptop then unplug it from the outlet. In case it was near the spillage area, make sure that you leave the cable with the plug (the one that goes into the laptop) hanging down, so that the water doesn’t get any further than it already did. Once again, do this as quickly as possible; if the water makes its way into the power outlet it could compromise your entire electrical grid.
  • Battery – the next step that you should take is to remove the battery, but make sure not to close the lid when you do that. Your laptop will keep certain parts powered even when it’s turned down, so removing the last remaining power source is advisable. However, if you don’t have the right kind of screwdriver or if you have a non-removable battery you can skip this step.
  • Other components – remove everything else that’s easy to unplug: mouse, keyboard, cards, USB cables. Furthermore, if you know how to and if it doesn’t take you too long to remove them, you should also take out the RAM and the hard drive. However, you should try to do this without moving your laptop around too much so that the water doesn’t reach any new areas.
  • Drying – take a towel and carefully, but thoroughly, dry the outside and the places that you can reach. Once that’s done put a dry towel on a table or on a clear surface, open the laptop as wide as you can and put it face down on the towel. Wait for about 6 hours, then if the laptop dried, try putting it back together and see if it will work. In case it doesn’t, take it to a professional and see if he can help you out.Rice can also work as an amazing drying agent.

Remember to isolate yourself from static discharge.
